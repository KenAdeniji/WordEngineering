Intestinal collapse, or more accurately bowel obstruction or intussusception, occurs when part of the intestine twists, folds like a telescope, or becomes blocked, stopping the passage of digested food. This life-threatening condition causes severe abdominal pain, vomiting, and bloating, requiring immediate medical care. Cedars-Sinai +2
Key Aspects of Intestinal Collapse (Bowel Obstruction/Intussusception)
What it is: A mechanical blockage where intestines collapse, often folding (intussusception) or twisting (volvulus).
Symptoms: Severe belly pain/cramping, vomiting, inability to pass stool or gas, abdominal swelling, and loud bowel sounds.
Causes: Scar tissue (adhesions) from surgery, hernias, tumors, or infections.
Treatment: Medical emergency, often requiring imaging (CT scan), intravenous fluids, and surgery to untwist or remove the blocked segment.
Risks: If untreated, it can cause tissue death, perforation, and severe infection (peritonitis). Cleveland Clinic +5
Synonyms/Related Conditions
Intussusception: A "telescoping" of the intestine.
Volvulus: A twisting of the intestine.
Bowel obstruction: General term for blocked intestines.
Paralytic ileus: Functional obstruction where movement stops. AMBOSS +2
Disclaimer: If you or someone else is experiencing severe abdominal pain, seek immediate emergency medical care.
