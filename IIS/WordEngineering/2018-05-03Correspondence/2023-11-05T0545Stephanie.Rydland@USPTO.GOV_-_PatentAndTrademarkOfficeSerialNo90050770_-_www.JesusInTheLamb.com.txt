LEGAL ASSISTANCE & SCAM AWARENESS:

The trademark process is very legal in nature, some choose to file on their own and some seek legal assistance. Please be careful when dealing with 3rd party companies and Firms do to the many scams that are currently going on. We have provided several safe options below. Visit our 'Scam Awareness' page for more information at https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/protect/scam-awareness .

You can consult the American Bar Association’s webpage at http://americanbar.org/legalservices/findlegalhelp/home.cfm for U.S.-licensed attorney referral information, or conduct an internet search to find attorneys specializing in trademark or intellectual property law.  If you need free or reduced-fee legal assistance from a U.S.-licensed attorney with a matter before the USPTO, you may want to consider the Law School Clinic Certification Program, a local bar association, or the INTA pro bono program.

USPTO LAW SCHOOL CLINIC CERTIFICATION PROGRAM:

The USPTO Law School Clinic Certification Program allows law students to practice intellectual property law before the USPTO under the strict guidance of a law school faculty clinic supervisor.

The schools participating in the Law School Clinic Certification Program provide services on a pro-bono basis to those that qualify, but each school has its own criteria for accepting clients.  We do not endorse any one law school clinic participating in the clinic program.

For more information on the program, including a list of participating schools, visit https://www.uspto.gov/learning-and-resources/ip-policy/public-information-about-practitioners/law-school-clinic-1 .

U.S. BAR ASSOCIATION:

Attorney state bar associations often have pro bono divisions that provide free legal assistance to those who cannot otherwise afford such services.  Each state bar association has its own range of available pro bono services and its own criteria for accepting pro bono clients.

For more information on pro bono services available to you, contact your local state attorney bar association.  To locate a bar association in your area, visit https://www.americanbar.org/groups/intellectual_property_law/resources/probonostates.html .

INTA PRO BONO SERVICES:

The International Trademark Association (INTA) has also established a pilot program created to connect eligible individuals and non-profit organizations in need of trademark legal assistance with U.S.-licensed trademark attorneys who are looking for pro bono opportunities. Information on this program can be found at: http://www.inta.org/Advocacy/Pages/ProbonoClearinghouse.aspx .
